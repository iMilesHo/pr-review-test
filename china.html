Introduction to China

Overview

China, officially the People's Republic of China (PRC), is the world's most populous country and the third largest by area. Located in East Asia, it covers approximately 9.6 million square kilometres.

Key Facts

  • Capital: Beijing
  • Population: ~1.4 billion
  • Official Language: Mandarin Chinese (Putonghua)
  • Currency: Renminbi (RMB / Yuan ¥)
  • Government: Unitary one-party socialist republic

History

China has one of the world's oldest continuous civilizations, with a recorded history spanning over 4,000 years. It was home to several dynastic empires, including the Han, Tang, Song, Ming, and Qing dynasties. The People's Republic of China was established on 1 October 1949.

Culture

China has a rich cultural heritage that includes traditional arts such as calligraphy, painting, and opera, as well as martial arts (wushu / kung fu), tea culture, and festive celebrations like the Chinese New Year and the Mid-Autumn Festival.

Economy

China has the world's second-largest economy by nominal GDP and the largest by purchasing power parity (PPP). It is a major global manufacturing hub and a leading exporter of goods worldwide.
